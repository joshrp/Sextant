# Scoring Methods

When your factory has multiple valid solutions that meet your goals, the solver uses a scoring method to choose the "best" one. Different scoring methods prioritize different objectives.

The solver doesn't change anything about your recipes, connections or goals. It selects which factories to run and which to not run based on whether it reduces the targetted score. 

This works best when you set up multiple options for resources. The welcome factory has an example of this with both steel and iron available for making mechanical parts.

Switch between Lowest use of inputs & Infrastructure to see the difference.

### Lowest Use of Inputs
Minimizes the total consumption of input resources, includes inputs listed in the sidebar. Includes a small fraction of infrastructure costs as a tie breaker. 

### Lowest Use of Infrastructure
Minimizes the total infrastructure requirements.

### Footprint
Minimizes the physical space used by buildings. 

### Outputs
Maximizes the total production of all outputs. 
This is disabled for now as it is hard to prevent or detect infinite loops where there is nothing restrciting the output.
